Joe Pass

virtuoso of modern jazz guitar

A time line of the life and musical career of Joe Pass:

  • January 13, 1929 - born Joseph Anthony Jacobi Passalacqua in New Brunswick, New Jersey
  • 1943 - active playing career started at age 14
  • 1950s - developed a heroin addiction and spent much of the decade in prison
  • 1960s - Pacific Jazz label made early classics Catch Me, 12-String Guitar, For Django, and Simplicity; mostly did TV (The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson, The Merv Griffin Show, The Steve Allen Show) and recording session work in LA
  • 1963 - Received Downbeat magazine's "New Star Award."
  • 1965 - toured with pianist George Shearing
  • 1970s - played and recorded with Benny Carter, Milt Jackson, Herb Ellis, Zoot Sims, Duke Ellington, Dizzy Gillespie, Ella Fitzgerald, Count Basie, and others under Pablo Records label
  • 1974 - landmark solo album Virtuoso released

  • 1975 - The Trio won Grammy Award for Best Jazz Performance by a Group
  • 1973-1986 - Pass and Ella Fitzgerald record six albums together
  • 1994 - dies in LA of liver cancer
